  • Videos

    Videos are meant to be user-friendly and a great way to share information. But deaf and hard-of-hearing people can’t fully benefit from your videos unless they are captioned or transcribed. Some people think that automatic captioning--such as YouTube’s--makes videos accessible to a deaf and hard-of-hearing audience. Check out some automatic captioning with the sound off. Observe. Then contact CaptionAccess to get that captioning right. Every time.

  • Websites

    Many corporate websites teem with videos and audio clips that deaf and hard-of-hearing people can’t understand. They make up a fifth of the U.S. population and are as interested in your website media as anyone else. By making this media accessible via captioning you’ll tap into a whole new audience, one predisposed to become regular customers because you provide the accessibility they need. Isn’t that what marketing is all about?

  • Archived Media

    Some organizations archive media on their website and make it available only by a log-in process and sometimes for a fee. But it’s archived for a reason: to be used. Without captioning, deaf and hard-of-hearing people cannot fully make sense of archived media. We’re big on media being useful. Contact CaptionAccess and get the most out of your archived media. And when you caption word will get around...
